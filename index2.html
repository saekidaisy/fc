       10, this is the years of time since China’s largest missing children website- “Go Back Home” was created. 70,000, this is the number of people who have registered on the website, which is equivalent to one in every 19,000 people who do not know who their biological parents are. According to the average family size in China, these children who do not know where they are now or who do not know where their family is, involve more than 4 million people. It must be noted that, however, this is only the number that is known to be registered.
       In the past ten years, we have witnessed the establishment of the national DNA database against abduction. The Ministry of Public Security’s “Emergency Release Platform for Children’s Missing Information” was released. The legal explanation for the highest sentence of death for human traffickers seems to be a little bit of hope for families to find lost children.
       Is that true?

Images of those who are looking for their family



       What's different from what we might think is that there are more female missing kids and people who are looking for their missing kids than those of male.

       It must be pointed out that most of the missing kids separated from the family when they were under one year old. As a result, they usually didn't remember anything about their family.

       They were so young when got missing and the time away from home was so long that for most children who left their family since childhood, finding their biological parent is a root-seeking obsession. Nevertheless, perhaps he and his family know it is nearly possible

       The runner of "Back to Home" Zhang Baoyan mentioned such a cruel reality in the interview: the majority of missing children will not return to their original family.

       According to the thesis, we can reach a consensus that there are some regions that the child abducting ratio is particularly high. Henan is recognized as a region with the highest incidence of abducting and buying kids. In addition to Henan, the overall trend is that the five major provinces of abducting are Yunnan, Sichuan, Guangxi, Guizhou and Xinjiang. Scholar Wang Xizhang's statistics on child abducting cases in Shandong Province from 2009 to 2014 found that the kidnapped children from Yunnan, Sichuan, Guangxi, and Guizhou made up 72.6% of the total known cases. The six provinces that suffered most from the inflow of abducted children are Guangdong, Zhejiang, Jiangsu, Fujian, Shandong, and Anhui. From the data I sampled, the same characteristics are also presented.


Nearly half of the children’s loss is not caused by abduction



       In the past, abducters were considered the chief culprit in getting children out of their families. But in fact, up to 47.2% of the children in the sample were abandoned or placed out for adoption by the parents. The proportion of adoption is 38.3%.
       In addition to the high-frequency appearance of words such as "too young" "infant" and "unclear", words related to each other such as "one-child policy" "over-birth" and "adoption" are eye-catching.
       Wang Xizhang’s statistics on the cases of child trafficking detected in Shandong Province in recent years are as high as 72% sold by relatives, while the traffickers who steal children by violent means only account for 1%. There are different reasons for parents to sell their children. There are probably some: the rural super-life can not afford to pay a fine, the unmarried pre-pregnancy does not have the ability to support, the contraceptives are born and cannot be raised, hope to send out to let the children be raised by the good people, and so on.

       The research by Chen Baifeng, Dong Leiming said that in some poverty-stricken areas, such as Shanxi Yinzhou, Sichuan Liangshan, Guangxi Yulin, Yunnan Honghe, Wenshan, and Zhaotong, there have been some people living on abducting children. There is even a saying that “If you want to be rich, you must have a big belly." what's more, there are also some “abduction villages”, and a half or even nearly 80% of the villagers have sold their children.

Better Conditions

       In fact, anti-abducting policies have long been made. In 2009, the national DNA database was established. In May 2016, the Criminal Investigation Bureau of the Ministry of Public Security established a national platform to fight against child abducting, making use of the Internet: Reunion - the Emergency Platform for Missing Children's Information. In December 2016, the Interpretation of the Supreme People's Court on Several Issues Concerning the Specific Application of Laws in the Trial of Crimes of Trafficking in Women and Children was promulgated, clearly stating that deception, inducement and other means for infants and young children to be removed from guardians or caregivers are regarded as criminal law. "Stealing infants and young children" as stipulated in Article 2-4, paragraph 1 (6). The judicial interpretation will take effect on January 1, 2017. Article 245 of the Criminal Law of China stipulates that those who abduct or sell a woman or a child shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of no less than five years and no more than ten years, and being fined. Those who steal children for the purpose of selling shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of no less than ten years or life imprisonment, and being fined or confiscated property. If the circumstances are particularly serious, the death penalty is imposed and the property is confiscated. This means that abducting children can be sentenced to death. In addition, the implement of the second-child policy has also helped improve the conditions. Chen Shiqu, director of the Criminal Investigation Bureau of the Ministry of Criminal Investigation, said in an interview with Zhongxin.com that they found that some people wanted to buy a child because of the one-child policy. Those who want to have a large number of children tend to buy children.
       Better conditions can be seen from the number of missing people: since 1932, the number of missing people increased steadily, with a small soar between 1959 and 1960, reached its peak around 1990, and has gradually decreased since then. Although there has been rising some in recent years, it is much better than before. The successful cases of each year also show a good trend year by year. We are getting closer and closer to the beautiful vision of “no kidnapping in China”.



More Efforts Required



               Although the trend of finding family is moving in the direction we are looking forward to, the number of missing children is getting smaller and smaller, we still have to admit that in the face of a huge base, the number of successful cases seems to be a drop in the bucket – the overall recovery ratio is only 4.3%, which means that there are only 4 lost 100 children. Being able to find their home, cruelly, for the four children and their families, the reunion has nearly half the possibility that it will take place 20 to 30 years after they and their parents respectively.
        The situation is not supporting. After analyzing 2,801 successful cases, we found that even if one case of missing children does not occur in 2018, there is a 91% chance of helping all children find their family going to 2338, which is 321 years later.
